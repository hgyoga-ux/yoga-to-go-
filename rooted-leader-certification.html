About the Program

Built from the
inside out

The Rooted Leader Certification is the train-the-trainer pathway for people who want to bring embodied, nervous-system-aware programming into their communities. It assumes no prior facilitation experience — only a genuine calling to work with young people and a willingness to do the inner work that good facilitation requires.

The course is built from the inside out. Trainees first move through core content as participants — because you cannot guide someone somewhere you have not been. From there, the course builds outward: adolescent development, trauma-informed practice, facilitation skills, curriculum delivery, and a live practicum.

Fifty hours is enough time to develop the competence and the confidence to hold this space well. Certification is the beginning of an ongoing practice — and of membership in the Rooted Leader community.

Certification Pathway

Three steps to
certification

Complete All Eight Modules
Move through 50 hours of online, self-paced learning — experiencing the content as a participant before stepping into the facilitator role.
Submit Your Delivery Plan
Design a complete facilitator plan for one full session, adapted for a specific community context of your choosing.
Complete the Live Practicum
Lead a 30-minute facilitated segment with peers as participants. Receive structured mentor feedback. Earn your certification.

Program Structure

Eight modules.
One transformation.

01
3 Hours
Foundations of the Rooted Leader
Establish the philosophical and relational ground from which every facilitator works. Before learning how to lead, trainees explore who they are as leaders.
02
7 Hours
The COSC Experience — From the Inside
Move through the full COSC Stress Continuum as a participant first — every zone, every breath practice — before being asked to lead them.
03
7 Hours
The Anatomy Focus — From the Inside
Build embodied understanding of the body systems underpinning The Quiet Breath before stepping into the facilitator seat.
04
5 Hours
Adolescent Development
Understand how the adolescent brain and nervous system actually work — as a practical lens that makes facilitators more patient and more effective.
05
8 Hours
Trauma-Informed Facilitation
Develop a trauma-informed lens that shapes every choice — from how you open a session to how you respond when something unexpected surfaces.
06
8 Hours
Facilitation Skills & Group Dynamics
Build the practical skills of holding a group — the art of reading a room, holding a thread, and adapting in real time.
07
6 Hours
Delivering the Curriculum
Walk through the full program from the facilitator's seat — session by session, decision by decision, with room for community-specific adaptation.
08
6 Hours
Practicum & Certification
A supported final practice with real feedback and a clear pathway to certification — not a performance, but the beginning of your practice.
Total Program Hours 50 Hours

Who This Is For

Called to work
with young people

Youth Workers & Educators
People already in relationship with young people who want to add embodied, regulation-focused tools to their existing practice.
Community Organizers & Faith Leaders
Those serving youth in faith-based, community, and non-profit settings where relational safety and holistic wellbeing are central.
Coaches & Helping Professionals
Coaches, mentors, and practitioners who want a structured, trauma-informed framework for working with adolescents in groups.

No yoga background required. No clinical training required. What is required is a regulated nervous system, a non-shaming posture toward young people, and a genuine commitment to the inner work this role asks of you.
