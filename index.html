About

The Story Behind the Work

Leadership formation from the inside out.

Yoga to Go began as a mobile yoga practice — bringing movement, breath, and presence directly into communities, workplaces, and homes. Over more than two decades, that work evolved into something deeper: the recognition that the most important thing a leader can cultivate is not a skill set, but a regulated self.

This platform is built on that conviction.

The methodology draws on yoga therapy — a whole-person approach to regulation that integrates body, breath, and awareness, and considers individual uniqueness — evidence-based breath reeducation rooted in the Buteyko approach, and the COSC Stress Continuum, the same framework used by the US Navy and Marine Corps to identify and manage stress responses under pressure.

It is not a wellness program. It is leadership formation from the inside out. The work is offered with warmth, rigour, and deep respect for the communities you serve.

The Mentoring

What to Expect

One-on-one and group programs, built for the way you lead.

We mentor the whole leader — not just the strategy, the structure, or the skill set, but the nervous system underneath all of it. Because a regulated leader builds a regulated community. And regulation begins with the breath.

No time added to your day that isn't earned back in clarity and presence.

The Grounded Leader

The Foundation Beneath the Mentoring

Physiology is not separate from leadership. It is the ground it stands on.

Every mentoring relationship at Yoga to Go rests on a single physiological premise:

"You lead well from a steady centre."

Not consistently. Not sustainably. Not in the moments that matter most.

Chronic exhaustion, reactive communication, the inability to be fully present even when you want to be — these are not character flaws. They are physiological states. And physiological states can be retrained.

The Quiet Breath — nasal, light, slow, and low in the body — is the key tool. It is invisible, immediate, and available in any moment: before a difficult conversation, during a board meeting, in the hallway before you walk into your congregation.

This is not breathwork. It is breath reeducation — the systematic retraining of patterns that have been quietly sustaining your stress state without your knowledge.

The Outcome

H.E.R. — Hardiness, Endurance, Resilience — built through consistent practice — the trained ability to return to calm quickly, without losing your edge.
